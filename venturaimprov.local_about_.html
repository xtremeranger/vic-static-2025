<h2>Mission</h2>
<p>Ventura Improv Company exists to enrich people’s lives through improv theatre, and to nurture local talent and the cultural life of Ventura.</p>
<h2>Vision</h2>
<p>Ventura Improv Company hopes to create a thriving improvisational international center that gives people the necessary tools to communicate, play and understand themselves and the world in a holistic, more powerful way.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In 1989, Barbara Scott from Bay Area TheatreSports™ came to visit her mother in Santa Paula and while there she put on an improv workshop. At that time, there were a handful of performers doing improv in Ventura, and several of them (including Sally and Tom Mueller and Gary Best) attended that workshop. They considered short-form competitive improv “a revelation,” and went on to form a local <a href="https://impro.global/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">TheatreSports</a>™ league.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In the decades since then, the group has grown in size and scope. In 1999, the group took on the lease in the historic Livery in downtown Ventura, building a permanent stage and green room and a 80-person capacity audience seating area. In 2008, Ventura Area TheatreSports™/Livery Theatre changed its name to Ventura Improv Company. In January 2017, the VIC moved on from 34 North Palm Street. The group now performs and holds classes at </span> <a href="https://www.nambaarts.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Namba Performing Arts Space</a>, as well as holds special events and shows throughout Ventura County.</p>
<p>VIC is a 501(c)3 nonprofit organization.</p>
